The Odyssey
is thought to have been written between 800 and 700 BC by
Homer,
and was translated from Homeric Greek into English blank verse in 1871 by
William Cullen Bryant.

This Greek-names edition was prepared in 2026 by
Stoil M. Stoilov
as a companion to the audiobook narrated by an artificial voice modeled on
Sir Michael Caine
(ElevenLabs, 2026).

In this edition, the names of the gods and heroes have been restored to their Greek forms⁠—reading Odysseus, Poseidon, Athena, and Zeus where Bryant wrote Ulysses, Neptune, Minerva, and Jove⁠—throughout all twenty-four books, to match the names spoken in the narration. A short introduction transcribed from the narration has been added, an editor’s note describing the changes has been placed at the front, and Bryant’s original preface⁠—which argued for keeping the Latin names⁠—has been moved to the back of the book.

This edition is derived from the
Standard Ebooks edition
of Bryant’s translation, produced by Emma Sweeney and the Standard Ebooks volunteers,
which was based on public-domain page scans from the
Internet Archive, the HathiTrust Digital Library, and Google Books.
